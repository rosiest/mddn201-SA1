Internet Infrastructure

The internet consists of a tangible physical system so that we are able to move information from one place to another. Internet infrastructure is the physical aspects of how information is communicated and can come in numerous forms. Sometimes, these physical things aren’t visible to the human eye simply because they’re unnoticeable. The internet is able to ship binary information through things such as wires, cables, etc. However, all over Wellington, these can be in the forms of, but not limited to, antennae, CCTV cameras, manhole covers and distribution boards.

“We never, ever in the history of mankind have had access to so much information so quickly and so easily.”- Vinton Cerf

Antenna

Antennae are typically found on higher platforms and are used to transmit or receive signals. In this case, the antennae in the images below are used for internet infrastructure as this enables various wireless networking. In Wellington, I saw antennae mainly on top of buildings in the CBD area.

CCTV Cameras

CCTV (closed-circuit television) cameras are a type of security through the use of video surveillance. These cameras transmit signals to a specific place, usually monitors that are able to be viewed. These were typically found both outside and inside of Wellington stores, highly likely to monitor possible misdemeanour.

Manhole Covers

Most people do not know this, but to provide more connections to networks, a lot of physical forms of internet infrastructure can be located below us. These underground cables are used for the communication of data to and from different places, whether it’s local or international. Manhole covers are passages to these cables so that they are easily accessible if some things were to go wrong.

Distribution Board

Distribution boards divide electrical power into subsidiary circuits as part of an electricity supply system to provide power for our internet systems. These are where the electrical supply is distributed from so that buildings and houses can have access to power. Distribution boards were mainly located outside of buildings in Wellington in lots of different forms.
