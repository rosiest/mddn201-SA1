Internet Infrastructure

The internet consists of a tangible physical system so that we are able to move information from one place to another. Internet infrastructure is the physical aspects of how information is communicated and can come in numerous forms. Sometimes, these physical things aren’t visible to the human eye simply because they’re unnoticeable. The internet is able to ship binary information through things such as wires, cables, etc. However, all over Wellington, these can be in the forms of, but not limited to, antennae, CCTV cameras, manhole covers and power boxes.

Antennas

Antennae are typically found on higher platforms and are used to transmit or receive signals. In this case, the antennae in the images below are used for internet infrastructure as this enables various wireless networking. In Wellington, I saw antennae mainly on top of buildings in the CBD area.
